What you can do to avoid a scam

Never visit links sent by drivers

Some fake drivers could try to make you pay outside our app by sending a link, a phone number or an email address.

Be aware of weird behaviour and requests

Experienced drivers know they should not ask for money outside the platform. If someone asks you to do so, politely decline.

Only pay for your ride on our app

Even if the website sent by fake drivers looks like ours, do not transfer money outside our app.

How scammers try to get your money or payment details

By building a fake website that looks like ours

Scams succeed because they look like the real thing. Scammers are getting smarter: they will try to make you visit another website, very similar to ours, that will convince you to give them your money or payment details.

Make sure you’re always paying on SkopjeTransit’s app or website.

By creating fake user profiles

Fake profiles are usually created just a few days or hours ago and don’t have any ratings from other members. They also often charge less than other similar rides. Before or after booking the ride, they could try to contact you outside of our platform (through your phone or WhatsApp).

If you think you interacted with a fake profile, you can report it.

What to do if you think someone is trying to scam you

If the driver sends you a link to pay for your ride

You can reply: “I’m uncomfortable using the link you sent, I will follow SkopjeTransit’s recommendation and pay on their app.”

If the driver insists on getting paid in another way

You can reply: “Don’t worry, SkopjeTransit’s application is secure.”

If the driver asks you for additional charges

You can reply: “I have already paid for the ride on SkopjeTransit’s app.”

Noticed something suspicious? Report it.

Help us protect you from scams by reporting if you notice a suspicious message, if you have been scammed or if you think someone is trying to scam you.
